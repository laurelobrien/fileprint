Glossary of Terms

  • Perfect Binding — binding a book by aligning loose leafs along their intended spine and applying glue, usually with a brush or a glue knife. Thick magazines and soft-cover books are usually bound this way. A cover and even a dustjacket can be added.
  • Section — the "section" of paper you fold together like a taco to create facing pages. Usually this is for smythe-sewn binding but sometimes perfect binding too. Sections are stacked and sewn together or otherwise bound through their fold edges. Some forms of Japanese binding face the section fold towards the fore-edge of the book to keep pages double-layered, usually because of the use of extremely thin paper.
  • Fore-edge — the edge of a page that faces away from the spine. This is the edge you usually grab to turn the page.
  • Head — the top edge of a page.
  • Tail — the bottom edge of a page.
  • Media Query — a CSS statement that asks a browser what media it's serving content to, such as screen or print, and applies the requisite styles. Media queries can also specify device size such as (@media screen) and (max-width < 480px), meaning styles for a screen less than 480px wide
