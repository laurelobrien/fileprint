Triple Canopy

Triple Canopy is a web-first magazine that puts out print compendiums of its online content as a means of archival rather than as a primary format.

Flaneur

Flaneur, named after the French word for the modern, meandering "man of the streets", examines on one city-street each issue with a literary focus. It has a section of web-specific content called the Unprintables˚, taking advantage of the multi-media abilities of our computers.

Uncube

Uncube is a Berlin-based architectural magazine that puts out a monthly issue much like print mags, but keeps them entirely online. Articles can be browsed by thumbnail, an attractive feature of hyperlinks.

The Gourmand

The Gourmand, describing itself as a food & culture journal, asks readers to choose between pring and screen right from the homepage. The content is entirely different between the two, one taking advantage of full print spreads and the other image slideshows and videos.

The Binder and the Server

The Binder and the Server is an essay by senior editor of Triple Canopy Colby Chamberlain, published in № 4 of Art Journal's˚ 2011 volume. It is the result of discussion among members of Triple Canopy about how and why they tackle the medium of web publication, and what has changed since a generation raised with the internet took to their places in the publishing world.

Print is Dead. Long Live Print

Ruth Jamieson collected examples of independent magazines succeeding where Big Print suffers in a series of case studies titled Print is Dead. Long Live Print. Jamieson believes that print as we knew it for hundreds of years has been slain by the web, but its death clears the way for publications to be unconcerned with immediacy, universal appeal, or competing with the internet's torrent of information.
